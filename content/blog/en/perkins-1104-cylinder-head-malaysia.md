## What Is the Perkins 1104 Engine?

The Perkins 1104 is a four-cylinder, 4.4-litre diesel engine produced from the late 1990s through the 2010s. It comes in naturally aspirated (1104C-44), turbocharged (1104C-44T), and turbo-aftercooled (1104C-44TA) variants, producing from 60 to 130 horsepower.

In Malaysia, the 1104 is primarily found in Massey Ferguson tractors (MF240, MF350, MF375, MF385), as well as industrial generators, forklifts, and construction equipment. It succeeded the Perkins 1000 Series and shares many design elements with it.

## Common Perkins 1104 Cylinder Head Failures

The cylinder head is the 1104's most failure-prone major component. Malaysian workshops report three recurring issues:

### 1. Cracking Between Valve Seats

The most common failure mode. The 1104 cylinder head is cast iron, and thermal cycling over 10,000+ operating hours causes micro-cracks between the intake and exhaust valve seats. Once a crack propagates, coolant enters the combustion chamber — white smoke from the exhaust and pressurised coolant are tell-tale signs.

**Symptoms:**
- White exhaust smoke (coolant burning)
- Coolant loss without visible leaks
- Overheating under load
- Combustion gas in radiator (bubbles with cap off)

### 2. Head Gasket Failure

The 1104 head gasket is a multi-layer steel (MLS) design. Improper torque sequence during reassembly, or running the engine hot after coolant loss, will cause gasket failure between cylinders 2 and 3 — the hottest part of the head.

**Symptoms:**
- Oil in coolant or coolant in oil (milky appearance)
- Compression loss between adjacent cylinders
- External oil or coolant seepage at the head-block joint

### 3. Injector Sleeve Leaks

The 1104 uses copper injector sleeves pressed into the cylinder head. Over time these sleeves erode or loosen, allowing combustion gas or coolant to bypass. This is often misdiagnosed as a head gasket failure.

**Symptoms:**
- Fuel dilution in engine oil
- Black smoke under load
- Diesel smell in cooling system

### Identifying Your 1104 Variant

The engine serial number plate is riveted to the cylinder block (injection pump side). The build list number on the plate determines which head variant your engine needs. Common build list prefixes for the 1104 include:

- **RE, RG, RH** — industrial 1104C-44
- **NJ, NK** — agricultural (Massey Ferguson) 1104C-44T
- **PJ, PK** — turbo-aftercooled 1104C-44TA

Always quote your engine serial number when ordering. Cylinder heads are not interchangeable between 1104 variants — bolt patterns and injector angles differ.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How do I know if my Perkins 1104 cylinder head is cracked?**
A: Three signs: white smoke from the exhaust (coolant entering combustion), bubbles in the radiator with the engine running (compression gas entering coolant), and unexplained coolant loss without external leaks. A workshop pressure test confirms diagnosis.

**Q: Can a cracked Perkins 1104 head be repaired or does it need replacement?**
A: Minor cracks between non-critical areas (away from valve seats and injector bores) can sometimes be welded or stitched by a specialist machine shop. However, cracks between valve seats or into water jackets almost always require replacement. Have a machine shop pressure-test and crack-test before deciding.
