## Why are some tractor parts so hard to find in Malaysia?

Three main reasons:

1. **Discontinued models** — manufacturers stop producing parts for older models after 10-15 years. Kubota L-series tractors from the 1990s, Massey Ferguson models from the 2000s, and older Yanmar marine engines all have parts that are no longer in production.

2. **Limited local stock** — Malaysian dealerships stock what sells. Niche parts for less common models, low-volume variants, or specific aftermarket components are rarely held in local inventory.

3. **Single-distributor markets** — For brands like Massey Ferguson (the authorised MF distributor channel for Malaysia), you cannot shop around. If they don't have it, the part is effectively unavailable through official channels.

## What counts as a "hard to find" tractor part?

Any part that is not available through standard dealer channels in Malaysia:

- **Vintage/obsolete parts** — for tractors 10+ years old where manufacturers no longer produce the part
- **Niche engine components** — specific gasket sets, injector nozzles, piston rings for low-volume models
- **Hydraulic system parts** — custom seals, hoses, valves for older or less common systems
- **Transmission and drivetrain parts** — gears, bearings, shafts for models with limited parts support
- **Electrical components** — sensors, control units, wiring harnesses for discontinued models
- **Cooling system parts** — radiators, water pumps, thermostats for niche applications
